### Detailed Game Introduction

Unblock That is a classic and challenging sliding block puzzle. The game presents you with a grid filled with wooden blocks of various sizes. Your goal is to strategically slide these blocks to clear a path for the special red block to escape through the exit. ### Gameplay Strategy & Walkthrough

1.  **Identify the Main Blockers:** Look at the red block and identify the blocks that are directly in its path to the exit. Your first goal is to move these primary blockers.
2.  **Create Space:** To move a blocking piece, you often need to move other pieces first to create empty space. Look for smaller blocks that can be shuffled around easily to open up pathways.
3.  **Think Several Moves Ahead:** Don't just make random moves. Try to visualize the sequence of moves required to clear the path. The best players think several steps ahead to solve the puzzle in the fewest moves possible.
4.  **Don't Be Afraid to Reset:** If you get into a situation where the blocks are completely jammed and you can't see a solution, sometimes the best option is to reset the level and start again with a fresh approach.

<!-- i18n:section:controls -->
### Controls Guide

The game is played with simple `drag`-and-`drop` controls.

*   **Move a Block:** `Click` and `hold` on any wooden block. You can only slide it along its orientation (horizontally or vertically) into an empty `space`.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

* <!-- i18n:faq:id=faq:unblock-that:q-what-is-the-goal-ab58581b -->
  **Q: What is the goal of the game?**
    *   **A:** To slide the red block all the way to the right so it can exit the board.

* <!-- i18n:faq:id=faq:unblock-that:q-why-cant-i-move-adf98dbf -->
  **Q: Why can't I move a block?**
    *   **A:** Blocks can only move along their long axis (a vertical block moves up/down, a horizontal block moves left/right). Make sure there are no other blocks in its path.

* <!-- i18n:faq:id=faq:unblock-that:q-how-is-the-score-eb504d39 -->
  **Q: How is the score determined?**
    *   **A:** Your performance is usually rated by the number of moves you take to solve the puzzle. The fewer moves you use, the better your score or star rating will be.
